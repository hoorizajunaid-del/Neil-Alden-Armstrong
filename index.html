About Neil Armstrong

Neil Alden Armstrong (August 5, 1930 – August 25, 2012) was an American astronaut, aeronautical engineer, and naval aviator. He became the first human to walk on the Moon during NASA’s Apollo 11 mission in 1969, marking one of the greatest achievements in human history.Armstrong was born near Wapakoneta, Ohio. He developed an early interest in aviation and earned his pilot’s license at the age of 16. He later studied aeronautical engineering at Purdue University, with his education sponsored by the United States Navy.After serving as a naval aviator in the Korean War, Armstrong became a test pilot for NACA (later NASA), flying high-speed experimental aircraft including the X-15. In 1962, he joined NASA’s Astronaut Corps.On July 20, 1969, Armstrong stepped onto the Moon and famously said, “That’s one small step for a man, one giant leap for mankind.”

    Biography of Neil Armstrong

    • Born on August 5, 1930, in Wapakoneta, Ohio, USA.
    • Took his first airplane ride at the age of six.
    • Earned his student pilot’s license at age 16.
    • Studied aeronautical engineering at Purdue University.
    • Served as a naval aviator during the Korean War.
    • Became a test pilot for NACA (later NASA) in 1955.
    • Joined NASA’s Astronaut Corps in 1962.
    • Commanded the Gemini 8 mission in 1966.
    • Became the first person to walk on the Moon in 1969.
    • Passed away on August 25, 2012, at the age of 82.
